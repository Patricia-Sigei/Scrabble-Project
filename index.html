Scrabble Board Game - Comprehensive Guide

Overview

Scrabble is a popular word game where players use letter tiles to form words on a 15x15 board. Each letter has a specific point value, and players score points based on the words they create and the bonus squares they cover. The game ends when all tiles are used or no valid moves can be made. The player with the highest score wins.

Scrabble Tutorial - Learn by Playing

Welcome to Scrabble! Start by moving a tile from your rack.

Game Instructions

  1. Each player draws tiles to determine who goes first.
  2. Each player starts with 7 letter tiles on their rack.
  3. The game board is a 15x15 grid with special bonus squares.
  4. Agree on a dictionary to resolve challenges.

The first player must place a word using the center square, which is marked with a star. Words must be at least two letters long.

Example: If the first player plays the word "CAT", the letter "A" must be on the center star.

Players take turns forming words horizontally or vertically. Each word must connect to an existing word.

Example: If "CAT" is already on the board, you can add "S" to form "CATS".

After each turn, players draw tiles from the tile bag to ensure they have 7 tiles. If no tiles remain, continue playing with the remaining tiles.

Players earn points by adding the values of each letter in their word, plus any bonuses from premium squares.

Example: If you place "DOG" with "D" on a double-letter score (DL) and "O" on a triple-word score (TW):
  • D (2 points) becomes 4 points (double-letter score).
  • O (1 point), G (2 points), total = 4 + 1 + 2 = 7 points.
  • The word score is tripled: 7 x 3 = 21 points.

Game Rules

  • All words must be horizontal or vertical. Diagonal words are not allowed.
  • Words must connect with existing words on the board.
  • Proper nouns, abbreviations, and prefixes or suffixes are not allowed.
  • Challenged words are checked in the agreed-upon dictionary.

If a player believes an opponent's word is invalid, they can challenge it. If the word is valid, the challenger loses their turn. If not, the player must remove the word and lose their turn.

How to Score Points

Each letter has a specific point value:

  • 1 point: E, A, I, O, N, R, T, L, S, U
  • 2 points: D, G
  • 3 points: B, C, M, P
  • 4 points: F, H, V, W, Y
  • 5 points: K
  • 8 points: J, X
  • 10 points: Q, Z

Scrabble boards feature special squares that can double or triple the value of letters or words:

  • Double Letter Score (DL): Doubles the point value of the letter.
  • Triple Letter Score (TL): Triples the point value of the letter.
  • Double Word Score (DW): Doubles the total word score.
  • Triple Word Score (TW): Triples the total word score.
Example: If you place the letter "C" (3 points) on a DW square and the total score for "CAT" is 5 points, your final score will be 5 * 2 = 10 points.

If a player uses all 7 tiles in one turn, they receive a 50-point bonus, called a "bingo".
